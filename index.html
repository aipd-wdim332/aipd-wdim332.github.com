About

The Art Institute of Portland is hosting a panel discussion about best practices in interactive team management. Industry professionals from a variety of backgrounds will speak on topics including intra-team communications, project workflow strategies, managing client relationships, how to assemble and retain the ideal interactive team, and balancing budget vs. quality. Admission is free, attendance is priceless.

Panelists

Tyler Sticka
Creative Designer
tylersticka.com

Tyler Sticka loves making websites, games, apps and iconography. He currently leads a tight-knit team of designers and illustrators at a stealth startup. Before that, he designed and built lots of neat stuff for Microsoft, Sasquatch! Music Festival, Waggener Edstrom, McAfee, Providence Health and other fine organizations. Tyler is also the design half of Backabit, a two-man shop that makes iOS games like Ramps and Lotsa Blocks. Wired contributing editor Steve Silberman called Tyler "a software poet."

Damon Gallaty
Senior Software Engineer
www.mcafee.com

I had my first computer, an Apple II+, when I was 10, and knew that I wanted to be a programmer from the time I learned BASIC on it. My first programming job was writing microcontroller firmware for an industrial company. On the side, I wrote a plug-in to add PGP encryption to Eudora Mail, which wound up getting me a job in Silicon Valley during the dot-com boom. PGP was acquired by McAfee, then sold off during the dot-com bust, but I managed to stay on with McAfee, and I’ve been writing security software there ever since. I’ve been with the company for 15 years and have learned a variety of programming languages, software development methods, and project management styles.
