PDX Interactive

An Open Discussion on Managing Interactive Teams

 
 

The Art Institute of Portland hosted a panel discussion about best practices in interactive team management. Industry professionals from a variety of backgrounds spoke on topics including intra-team communications, project workflow strategies, managing client relationships, how to assemble and retain the ideal interactive team, and balancing budget vs. quality.

Panelists

Tyler Sticka
Creative Designer
www.tylersticka.com

Tyler Sticka loves making websites, games, apps and iconography. He currently leads a tight-knit team of designers and illustrators at a stealth startup. Before that, he designed and built lots of neat stuff for Microsoft, Sasquatch! Music Festival, Waggener Edstrom, McAfee, Providence Health and other fine organizations. Tyler is also the design half of Backabit, a two-man shop that makes iOS games like Ramps and Lotsa Blocks. Wired contributing editor Steve Silberman called Tyler "a software poet."

Damon Gallaty
Senior Software Engineer
www.mcafee.com

Damon had his first computer, an Apple II+, when he was 10, and knew that he wanted to be a programmer from the time he learned BASIC on it. His first programming job was writing microcontroller firmware for an industrial company. On the side, he wrote a plug-in to add PGP encryption to Eudora Mail, which wound up getting him a job in Silicon Valley during the dot-com boom. PGP was acquired by McAfee, then sold off during the dot-com bust, but he managed to stay on with McAfee, and he's been writing security software there ever since. He's been with the company for 15 years and has learned a variety of programming languages, software development methods, and project management styles.

Shaun Tinney
Partner at The Good
www.thegood.com

Shaun Tinney is a Partner at The Good, a user-experience driven digital agency with a focus on effective ecommerce for Outdoor and Action Sports companies. He leads strategy and research efforts for the highly experienced team of design and development professionals at The Good, shepherding dot com launches for some of the largest sports brands in the world.

Randall Hansen
VP of User Experience
www.puppetlabs.com

Randall has been a design leader at consultancies and product companies in Portland for over ten years, and programming and designing for 30. He is passionate about creating high-quality, simple products that forge emotional connections with users, and about helping great people reach their full potential. Randall leads a user experience team of eight at Puppet Labs.

Chris Bloom
Designer & Developer
www.superstarmedia.com

Chris is an avid design and development professional with a decade of experience under his belt. He's helped build and launch dozens of major websites including michaeljackson.com, ozzy.com and paulsimon.com. Chris eats Drupal and dreams Javascript while managing the Portland office of SuperStar Media. Outside of work, he teaches at the Art Institute while co-leading the Portland Drupal Users Group. Chris obsessively collects development frameworks and tools that he swears he'll get to use "for real" some day. He holds an MBA from Washington State University - but don't hold it against him.

Lorenzo Ciacci
Director of Product
www.theclymb.com

Race car driver. Philatelist. LARP enthusiast. Yarn fetishist. Beat-boxer. None of these things describe Lorenzo. After last count he has worked in and with over 26 agencies across this place called "Earth" and has been working in the interactive space for fifteen years in various capacities of designer, developer and strategist. From Disney to TOOL, Intel to Visa, he's transversed and created something that has at one time or another delighted, infuriated, or made you question what it is you were interacting with. He's deeply passionate about device agnosticism, content strategy, design patterns and management methodologies. Currently deeply ensconced at The Clymb, a flash sales ecommerce site for outdoor and adventure gear, as well as adventures themselves.

Dan Chapman
Javascript Developer
www.theclymb.com

Known to friends as Chappy, Dan Chapman has been remarkably lucky in regards to having his interests and whims take him to the far reaches of the interactive galaxy. He has made up for his lack of attention span with a genuine commitment to making sure that people love the work he does for them. "I am so touched that the students of AiPD's web program have chosen me as this conversation's moderator. The years I spent teaching and administering the program are some of my most cherished. The care and feeding of an interactive team is both art and science, not for the faint of heart. I am thoroughly looking forward to exploring this topic with the panelists."
